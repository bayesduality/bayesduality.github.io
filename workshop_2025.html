The 3rd Bayes-Duality Workshop 2025

About Program Participants Videos

This is the third edition of the Bayes-duality workshop focusing on the development of AI that learns adaptively, robustly and continuously, like humans. The main goal of the meeting is two folds. First, to showcase our research conducted at RIKEN-AIP (first phase ends in 2025), and, second, to discuss (and refine) the long-term goals of the JST-CREAST project and foster collaborations among the attendees. Part 1 and 2 of the workshop are open to public (through live streaming) and will feature talks, tutorials, and discussion by world-leading researchers working on related topics. Part 3 will be closed meetings exclusively for the collaborators of the Bayes-duality project.

  • Part 1 (June 23-24) Sessions on special topics at RIKEN AIP Nihonbashi
  • Part 2 (June 25-27) Invited Talks at RIKEN AIP Nihonbashi (streamed live on Zoom)
  • Part 3 (June 30-July 4) CREST-ANR closed meeting at LecTore Atami Koarachi in Atami.

Similarly to the previous edition, we hope that the proposed activities help attendees increase their research's impact, outreach and help them network. This workshop is supported by RIKEN-AIP subsidy budget and the JST CREST Grant JPMJCR2112 on “A new Bayes-duality principle for adaptive, robust, and lifelong learning of AI systems”.

In-person attendance is limited to RIKEN-AIP researchers and invited guests, but all talks in Part 2 (June 25-27) will be streamed live. Videos of both Part 1 and 2 will be made publicly available, given that the speakers, attendees, and organizers consent to it.
